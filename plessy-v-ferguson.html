Plessy v. Ferguson, 1896

[The fourteenth amendment, which establishes the universal rights of all natural-born US citizens] could not have been intended to abolish distinctions based upon color, or to enforce social, as opposed to political, equality, or a commingling of the two races upon terms unsatisfactory to either."
      -Text from the majority opinion in Plessy v. Ferguson, written by Justice Henry Billings Brown
