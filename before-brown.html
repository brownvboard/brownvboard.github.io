Before Brown

The Institution of Segregation

     Plessy v. Ferguson and "Jim Crow" laws cemented segregation in the South and prevented black Americans from action, political or otherwise. Plessy's use of "separate but equal"[1], exploitative sharecropping, and forces like the KKK and police largely kept black Americans suppressed and segregated across America, especially in the South [2].
(Bill of Rights Institute)

Methods of Oppression

  • Sharecropping was an exploitative system of tenant farming that kept black Americans perpetually in debt to white landowners
  • The Ku Klux Klan, a white supremacist group, terrorized black communities to keep them subjugated [3]
"Mississippi sharecroppers" (Eisenstaedt, 1936).

Justifications

Whites often justified their subjugation of black Americans by declaring the emotions and mental processes of black Americans to be inferior [4]. The consequences of this mindset are apparent in both Plessy and, surprisingly, Brown. 
[White Southerners] are not bad people. All they are concerned about is to see that their sweet little girls are not required to sit in school alongside some big overgrown Negroes."     
 - Dwight D. Eisenhower [5]
(Parks, 1956, obtained from the Gordon Parks Foundation)
[Black peoples'] griefs are transient. Those numberless afflictions, which render it doubtful whether Heaven has given life to us in mercy or in wrath, are less felt, and sooner forgotten with them. In general, their existence appears to participate more of sensation than reflection."
     —Thomas Jefferson, former president and slave owner [6]

Black Communities

It took a great deal of courage, resilience, and strength of character for African Americans to maintain their self-respect and battle the daily humiliation of Jim Crow.  The black church, self-help organizations, and men’s and women’s clubs... offered refuge, support, and protection [from Jim Crow]... The more cut off that black communities became ... the more possible it became for... black entrepreneurs to create viable businesses of their own..."
                     - Donna R. Braden [7]

The NAACP

      Since 1909, the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) has been pursuing lawsuits on behalf of black Americans in their long-running campaign against racial oppression. Brown ​was one such case [9].
The NAACP [originally] aimed to secure for all people the rights guaranteed in ... the United States Constitution. Accordingly, the NAACP’s mission was and is to ensure the political, educational, social and economic equality of minority group citizens of United States and eliminate race prejudice. The NAACP seeks to remove all barriers of racial discrimination through democratic processes."
                 -NAACP Mission Statement [10]
"NAACP chief counsel Thurgood Marshall, outside the Supreme Court..." -Lokke (Walker, 1953, obtained from Lokke, 2014)
